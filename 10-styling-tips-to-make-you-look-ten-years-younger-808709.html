10 Styling Tips to Make You Look Ten Years Younger

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Friday, May 31, 2024

Age is just a number — what counts is how you feel on the inside. But whether we want to admit it or not, clothes have the power to boost our confidence and change the way we see ourselves. Knowing some crucial tricks and shortcuts to youthful appearance can help you use fashion to your advantage. And even more importantly, have a great time while experimenting with various outfit combinations.

We at Bright Side did thorough research to bring you the latest 10 tips from fashion experts that can make you look a decade younger.

1. Update your jeans

Dylan Travis/ABACA/Abaca/East News, Dylan Travis/ABACA/Abaca/East News

Old-fashioned jeans make you look out of touch, so stay updated on the current shapes, silhouettes, lengths, and colors of the jeans. Of course, the cut will depend on your figure, but if you want to appear youthful, keep an eye on what’s on-trend. If you have an hourglass shape, high-waisted jeans would be a fantastic option for you, as they draw attention to your narrow waist and help trim the hips. A pair of high-rise distressed jeans with minimal stretch will look super-flattering and on-trend. As a general rule, avoid jeans with too many embellishments as it may make the outfit look outdated.

2. Chose structured clothes

Dylan Travis/ABACA/Abaca/East News, Laurent VU/SIPA/SIPA/East News

Yes, we agree shapeless and boxy jackets and coats are very trendy and can look cool and flattering but only if you pair them with pieces that compliment your figure. Adding a single tailored piece to an outfit will add definition to your silhouette and make you look taller, fitter, and more flattering. It can be a blazer, jacket, or just an eye-catching, modern belt. For example, if you combine a tailored blazer or a coat with a classic pair of pants with a soft leg and a flowy blouse, you will notice that this single structured piece makes the whole outfit more youthful.

3. Wear brighter colors

Dylan Travis/ABACA/Abaca/East News, DDNY/Broadimage/EAST NEWS

When we say ’’bright’’ we don’t mean going overboard with pastels. According to some fashion gurus, wearing head-to-toe pastels can make you look less vibrant like you’re fading into the background. And that’s exactly what you want to avoid if you want to look younger. Instead, go for white, turquoise, or pink-violet.

Or, if you can’t resist the pastel tones, combine them with bolder colors or wear something black to add contrast. Also, don’t be afraid of experimenting with unexpected color combinations. Many style experts recommend wearing ’’weird’’ color combos since they can make you look fresh and fashion-forward.

4. Avoid matching accessories

Going for a matching jewelry set instead of a mixed ensemble could make you appear old-fashioned. The same goes for too many pieces of chunky jewelry and statement pieces. Instead, try breaking your jewelry sets up and go for jewelry that compliments each other. Pairing large necklaces with small rings, a big buckled belt with studded earrings, or combining jewelry made of different materials can make your outfit more up-to-date and fresh.

5. Pay attention to prints

AA/ABACA/EAST NEWS, Jef Hernandez/Everett Collection/East News

Bold, oversized prints can be beautiful and tempting, but very tricky to wear. What’s more, an oversized print will make you appear larger, which can make you seem older than you actually are. On the other hand, smaller, modern prints can make you look more youthful. The same is true for monochromatic outfits unless they are in pastel colors.

6. Add a modern twist to retro pieces

Unless you want to look like you just escaped from a historical movie set, avoid wearing all-vintage ensembles. The head-to-toe vintage look can seem especially strange in the modern office setting. The goal is to show off your ability to mix retro and modern, not to look older than you are. A vintage scarf, retro sunglasses, or a cute vintage hairpiece can look fabulous when combined with contemporary pieces; otherwise, it can make you look dated.

7. Get the skirt length right

NPA/The Grosby Group/Grosby Group/East News, Michael Tran/Sipa USA/East News, Invision/Invision/East News

In general, most women look best in a skirt or dress that sits slightly above, in the middle, or slightly below the knee. The hemline should end at the slimmest part of these three points. If you are petite, a knee-length skirt may not be the right choice for you, but an asymmetrical hemline can make you seem taller. On the other hand, shorter ladies may look taller in slightly shorter lengths. If you’re a fan of maxi skirts, make sure to style them right to avoid looking frumpy.

8. Stay away from jersey

Yes, jersey is comfortable and it can look attractive on a hanger, but it can make you look bigger and add years to your appearance. If you’re aiming to accentuate your curves, there are right ways to do it that don’t involve this flimsy fabric that wrinkles and latches on your body. If you want to look sophisticated and youthful in tight jersey clothes, you’d better think about getting some comfortable, good-quality shapewear. Another option is to layer it and add a tailored jacket or coat on top.

9. Choose the right eyewear

Choosing a pair of glasses takes some research and knowledge. Most people think that wearing any kind of reading glasses will make them look older no matter what style they choose, so they often opt for the ’’invisible’’ ones. However, rimless or wire-rimmed glasses can actually make you look older. Their minimal design won’t cover up much of your baggy eyes or wrinkles.

Before purchasing a rimless pair, try on a transparent one instead. The shape and the color will hugely depend on your face shape, as well as your hair and skin color, so take your time when buying glasses and choose your next pair wisely.

10. Go for higher heels

Birdie Thompson/AdMedia/Sipa USA/East News, NPA/The Grosby Group/EAST NEWS

We all know that shoes can make or break an outfit, but choosing a perfect pair can also make you appear younger. According to style experts, outdated or overly casual footwear can make you look frumpy and older, regardless of what you’re wearing. Of course, that doesn’t mean you can’t look fresh and put together in a trendy pair of sneakers.

But if you want to elongate your silhouette and appear slimmer, go for something with a higher heel. If you’re a fan of low heels or flats or low heels, try swapping them for a pointed-toe pump, for example. This little trick will make your favorite outfit more sophisticated and flattering.
